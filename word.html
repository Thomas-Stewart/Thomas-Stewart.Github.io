    • Prepared For The Calling

  • 2/1/15

    "While walking by the Sea of Galilee, he saw two brothers, Simon (who is called Peter) and Andrew his brother, casting a net into the sea, for they were fishermen. And he said to them, 'Follow me, and I will make you fishers of men.' Immediately they left their nets and followed him. And going on from there he saw two other brothers, James the son of Zebedee and John his brother, in the boat with Zebedee their father, mending their nets, and he called them. Immediately they left the boat and their father and followed him."
    - Matthew 4:18-22

    I am extremely encouraged at the actions of the first disciples. They made the decision to leave everything in pursuit of this Jesus character. I don't know how much fame Jesus had acquired at this time, but it couldn't have been hardly any. It wasn't like these four fishermen were loitering around with nothing to do. They had lives. They had occupations. James and John were with their father. And immediately they left all of that in pursuit of Jesus.

    I was struck by a very interesting thought that can be very convicting for some: What were the disciples thoughts that morning? I mean, God incarnate called them to follow Him. This is such a life-changing experience. And I guarantee they treated it like any other morning. James got up (probably pretty early since that's when the best fishing is), had his "cup of coffee" so to speak, and it was just another day in the life. Peter may have very well been caught up with financial trouble and stress over his lack of catches the past month. It's ridiculous to realize that their normal lives were changed in an instant.

    That being said, there is no record of argument from them. The Word says they immediately got up and followed him. I want a mindset like that. Sometimes my scope of life is so incredibly small. I pay attention to the details that most of the time cause me unnecessary anxiety and stress. These guys weren't worried about their worries under a microscope. They sensed a bigger picture playing out, and they were able to leave their lives to pursue it.

    There were three guys that were guilty of thinking too small, which removed them from the greatest opportunities of their lives.
    "As they were going along the road, someone said to him, 'I will follow you wherever you go.' And Jesus said to him, 'Foxes have holes, an dbirds of the air have nests, but the Son of Man has nowhere to lay his head.' To another he said, 'Follow me.' But he said, 'Lord, let me first go and bury my father.' And Jesus said to him, 'Leave the dead to bury their own dead. But as for you, go and proclaim the kingdom of God.' Yet another said, 'I will follow you, Lord, but let me first say farewell to those at my home.' Jesus said to him, 'No one who puts his hand to the plow and looks back is fit for the kingdom of God.'
    - Luke 9:57-62

    It never says whether or not these men followed Jesus or not, but the message is clear. The kingdom of God waits for no excuses. There is a war to be fought, a battle to be won. And we are called to participate.

    I want to follow Jesus with a reckless abandon. Abandon in the sense of leaving behind the troubles of this world, and being more concerned with the salvation of souls. Every excuse can be made as to why I should stay in my comfortable bubble while I let others (or God) do the heavy lifting. I want to be prepared to make a move as soon as God calls me to something. No excuses, no exceptions, reckless abandon.

    • Blue Car or Red Car?

  • 1/18/15

    Does God know about every movement of an atom? Where it will travel? Whether it will bond with this or that atom? How many valence electrons it gains or loses in a particular time period?
    Yes. The real question is: does He care?

    I have heard it said that, "God cares about 'important things' like a person's salvation or sin, but He doesn't care about the little things. I can walk down to the car dealership and pick up a Honda in whatever shade I desire. God doesn't care about the color."

    I agreed that there are much more important things at hand than something as meaningless as a blue or black truck, but then I started to think about myself and when I find my interest glossing over a particular subject. See, when I don't care about something, sometimes it stems out of a place of uninterest, but often the reality is that I lack either love or time.

    I either have more important things to turn my concern toward, or I am lacking compassion. I am far too busy to concern myself with a colony of ants constructing their new fortress on my front lawn. What about the countless homeless people I pass on my way to work? The innumerable orphans, domestic and overseas, that could use significant aid. Why is it that I am more concerned with my to-do list than with ants or orphans? Self-absorption creeping into my heart.

    To be fair, I am limited. I could dedicate the rest of my life to ant watching or aiding orphans. The fact remains that I will never be able to witness all the ant hills on the planet. And I will surely be wanting in resources for a plethora of unaided orphans.

    I am constricted. God is not.

    God lacks neither time nor love. Therefore, I think His concern is aimed not only at the "important things", but also at the mundane, average, monotonous. He has planned every little detail that spun this hunk of space rock into motion, and keeps it spinning.

    "And he is before all things, and in him all things hold together."
    - Colossions 1:7

    Not only is He Creator, but He is also Sustainer. He is never surprised or regretful. He knits reality together in a beautiful fashion; all pain and suffering used to draw hearts to Him.

    So yes, I think God does care about what color car I drive. There are too many varying factors for it to be otherwise. God's plan concerns all of Creation. I believe He is concerned about the movement of every minute particle.

    That being said, I don't believe that we should follow suit. We are not omniscient nor omnipresent beings. We are eternal, but only linearly, and with a finite starting point. We should not lose sleep over car colors. We need to "Set [our] minds on things that are above, not on things that are on earth." (Colossians 3:2). We need to live with a sense of urgency. Who knows how long we will have? How am I going to spend my time, knowing it is in limited amount?

    "Yet you do not know what tomorrow will bring. What is your life? For you are a mist that appears for a little time and then vanishes."
    - James 4:14

    Be encouraged today that God is concerned with the little things so we don't have to be.
